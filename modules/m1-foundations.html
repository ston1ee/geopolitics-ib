Module 1: What is Politics?

🟢 Foundations of Political Science

1.1 Defining Politics

Politics is the process by which groups of people make collective decisions. It is often described as "who gets what, when, and how." In the IB curriculum, we look at politics through several lenses:

Core Concepts

  • Sovereignty: The supreme authority within a territory.
  • Legitimacy: The right to rule.
  • Power: The ability to effect change.
  • Authority: The legitimate use of power.

1.2 Political Theories

Realism

Focuses on state interests, security, and the anarchic nature of the international system. States are the primary actors.

Liberalism

Emphasizes cooperation, international law, and the role of non-state actors like the UN and NGOs.

1.3 Research Methods

Political science uses both qualitative and quantitative data:

  • Qualitative: Interviews, case studies, historical analysis.
  • Quantitative: Statistical data, polling, economic indicators.
