Module 3: Power & Ideology

🟡 The Currency of Geopolitics

3.1 Understanding Power

Power is the ability to influence others to get the outcomes you want. It is the central concept in international relations.

Hard Power

Uses military force or economic sanctions to coerce others. It is tangible and measurable.

Soft Power

The ability to attract and co-opt rather than coerce. Focuses on culture, political values, and foreign policies (Joseph Nye).

Smart Power

The strategic combination of both hard and soft power tools to achieve foreign policy goals effectively.

3.2 Political Ideologies

Ideologies provide a framework for understanding how society should be organized and how power should be distributed.

  • Liberalism: Emphasizes individual liberty, free markets, and international cooperation.
  • Realism: Focuses on state survival, national interest, and power competition.
  • Socialism: Advocates for social ownership and egalitarian distribution of resources.
  • Populism: A political approach that strives to appeal to ordinary people who feel that their concerns are disregarded by established elite groups.
