Module 2: State & Sovereignty

🏛️ The Building Blocks of the International System

2.1 The Concept of the State

A state is a political entity that possesses a permanent population, a defined territory, a government, and the capacity to enter into relations with other states (Montevideo Convention).

Four Requirements of a State

  1. Defined Territory
  2. Permanent Population
  3. Effective Government
  4. Sovereignty (Capacity for Foreign Relations)

2.2 Types of Sovereignty

  • Internal Sovereignty: Supreme authority within the state's borders.
  • External Sovereignty: Recognition by other states and independence from outside interference.

2.3 Westphalian System

The Peace of Westphalia (1648) established the modern principle of state sovereignty, marking the beginning of the international system of nation-states.

2.4 Governance Systems

Democracy

Power is vested in the people, typically through elected representatives and the rule of law.

Authoritarianism

Concentration of power in a single leader or elite group, often with limited political freedoms.
