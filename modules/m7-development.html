Module 7: Development & Global Inequality

🌱 Understanding Paths to Progress and Sustainability

7.1 Defining and Measuring Development

Development is a multi-dimensional process involving change from a less desirable to a more desirable condition.

  • Economic Development: Growth in wealth (GDP/GNI per capita).
  • Social Development: Improvements in health, education, and standard of living (HDI).
  • Sustainable Development: Meeting current needs without compromising future generations.

Key Indicators

HDI

Human Development Index (Life expectancy, Education, GNI per capita).

Gini Coefficient

Measures income inequality within a state.

7.2 Theories of Development

Scholars debate why some states are more developed than others:

  • Modernization Theory: Suggests states follow a linear path through stages of growth.
  • Dependency Theory: Argues that global trade keeps the "periphery" dependent on the "core."
  • Neoliberalism: Focuses on free markets, deregulation, and privatization as keys to growth.

Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

17 goals set by the UN in 2015 to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure prosperity for all by 2030.
