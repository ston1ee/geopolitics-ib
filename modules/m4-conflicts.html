Module 4: Global Conflicts 2024-2026

🟡 Understanding Flashpoints and Crises

4.1 Nature of Conflict

Conflict is a state of disagreement or argument between people, groups, or countries. In geopolitics, we distinguish between different types of conflict:

  • Interstate Conflict: Between two or more states (e.g., Ukraine-Russia).
  • Intrastate Conflict: Within a single state (e.g., civil wars in Sudan or Myanmar).
  • Proxy War: A conflict where third parties fight on behalf of or alongside other parties.

Major Flashpoints 2026

Ukraine-Russia

Focus on sovereignty, territorial integrity, and NATO-Russia relations.

Israel-Gaza

Long-standing territorial dispute with significant humanitarian and regional implications.

Taiwan Strait

US-China rivalry over the status of Taiwan and maritime security in the South China Sea.

4.2 Conflict Resolution

Methods used to end conflicts peacefully:

  • Diplomacy & Negotiation
  • Mediation (Third-party involvement)
  • Sanctions (Economic pressure)
  • Peacekeeping (UN-led missions)
